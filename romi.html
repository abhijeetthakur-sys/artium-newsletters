Meerabai’s words. A melody composed in private and taught to one student. Carried for thirty years, corrected by a father, sung for the first time in a studio in Cooch Behar. Now on every platform in the world.

  ✦  

Romi was eight years old. She was sitting on a satranchi — one of those old woven carpets — in her guru’s teaching room, harmonium in front of her, learning a bhajan he had composed himself. Shri Shibdas Ghosh never wrote it down. Never recorded it. Never performed it outside that room. He taught it to Romi, and then he was gone. For the next thirty years, the only place this composition existed on earth was inside her memory.

Composed by

Shri Shibdas Ghosh

His composition was never written down. Never recorded. Never performed outside a private room. Until now.

The words are Meerabai’s — written five centuries ago, the kind of longing that doesn’t age. But the melody was her guru’s alone. Romi’s father, also his student, kept watch over the years — whenever a line slipped from her memory, he corrected it. When she finally stood in a studio in Haldibari, Cooch Behar and sang it into a microphone for the first time, she says it felt like an odd joy. “A song learned in childhood — now reaching people. This feels like God’s blessing.” It released on 8th May 2026 — Spotify, Apple Music, and 40+ platforms worldwide.

 

“We move through this world alongside people — but no one is more truly one’s own than God.”

— Romi Roy Chowdhury, on what Suno Dayal is actually about

Romi added something of her own. In the repetitions of the lines, she wove in her own emotional interpretation. It is faithful to what she learned, and it is entirely hers. This is Vol. I of the Artium Teacher Series.

A note from the Talent Department

When Romi told me she learned this song at age eight, sitting on a carpet in her guru’s room — and that for thirty years it lived nowhere except inside her — I kept thinking about how close it came to being lost. One life. One memory. That’s how thin the thread was. Artium Originals exists because that thread shouldn’t be that thin.

Every teacher in this company has music like this inside them. Songs they learned young, compositions their gurus gave them, things they’ve never sung in front of a microphone. We’re going to change that — one release at a time. Romi is first.
