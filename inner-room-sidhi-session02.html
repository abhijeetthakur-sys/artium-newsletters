What happened in Session 02 of the Carnatic Grooming Series by Sidhi Krishnamoorthy.

Session Mentor

Sidhi Krishnamoorthy

A sincere and dedicated Carnatic vocalist, she leads the Carnatic Grooming Series for the Artium Talent Cohort, nurturing aspiring musicians with depth, discipline, and artistry.

She didn’t start with an explanation. She sang. A Sankarabharanam alaapana, and on the final Sa, she deliberately went off-pitch. The room noticed instantly. Shriya said something was wrong. Kavya said it made her uncomfortable. Others nodded. No one needed to be told. They just felt it.

That became the session. Not a scale exercise, not a theory lecture. A single wrong note that proved everything. Pitch isn’t a technical detail. It is the emotional agreement between a singer and whoever is listening. Break it and the listener knows. They can’t always say why. But they know.

 

“Sruti Maata, Laya Pita. Lyrical mistakes can be forgiven. But not Sruti mistakes. When Sruti becomes natural, emotion travels directly to the listener.”

— Sidhi Krishnamoorthy

The second hour was a listening exercise. Sidhi put together clips from Carnatic, Hindustani, and Western singers. No screens, no annotation. Just the cohort with eyes closed and music playing. What they discovered was that when a singer is truly centred in sruti, you feel it the same way whether it’s a Carnatic alapana or a pop ballad. Genre doesn’t protect bad pitch. And it doesn’t block great pitch either.

A note from the Talent Department

The moment that stayed with me wasn’t a scale exercise. It was Sidhi singing a wrong note, and twelve students feeling it immediately, viscerally, before they could name what was off. That’s the thing about sruti. it bypasses the brain and goes straight to the gut. Great teaching is when a mentor can make you feel a concept before explaining it.
