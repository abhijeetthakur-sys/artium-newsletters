What happened in Session 04 of the Song Performance Lab by Jaydeep Bagwadkar.

Session Mentor

Jaydeep Bagwadkar

Vocal coach and music educator with a strong background training artists for commercial platforms. A mentor on multiple reality shows, JD leads the Song Performance Lab for the Artium Talent Cohort.

The session was filled with great energy and enthusiasm from all the students. JD brought the focus immediately to tempo identification: why every singer must develop this skill for accurate stage presentation. Not as a theory lesson, but through live demonstrations. How to find the original tempo of any song. How maintaining that tempo helps preserve the soul, groove, and feel of the composition. When the tempo shifts, something in the song quietly breaks, even if every note is right.

When the tempo shifts, something in the song quietly changes,for good or bad, that is a different thing.

Even if every note is right.

Another major part of the session was learning how to count musical bars during intros and interludes. These are the stretches most singers treat as empty time. JD showed, with practical demos, why bar counting during these passages is one of the most important skills for performing confidently and accurately on stage. He also introduced the Metronome app as a daily practice tool for building rhythm discipline and consistency over time.

One question from a student.
One redirect from the mentor.
A completely different way to practice.

The most decisive moment came from Ayaan. He mentioned that when he prepares a song, he listens to the interludes and, since he plays piano, starts memorising them straight away. JD gave him a clear direction: that is not the first step. Focus on the singing parts first. Learn them in detail. Build a complete script of the song. Only in a separate practice session should instrument work come in. It was a small correction that opened up a much larger conversation about how to practice. As homework, students were asked to pick three songs, identify their original tempos, and write down the musical bar structures.

 

"Discipline is the true rhythm of every musician. Before you perform for the world, learn to count your own musical bars with honesty, accuracy, and patience, because great performances are built long before the spotlight arrives."

Jaydeep Bagwadkar
