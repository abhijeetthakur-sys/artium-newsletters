The Night

unMute came to Hyderabad on 28th May. Seven performers, a warm brick-wall stage at Ameyaa Social, and a room that came ready to listen.
Hindi film songs. Telugu film songs. Carnatic classical. Students and teachers sharing the same stage. It was the kind of evening this programme was built for.

"Carnatic recitals. Hindi & Telugu film numbers. One stage, one night, one room full of people who didn't want it to end."

The range in that room was something. A young girl singing a Carnatic composition with so precision. Someone else doing a Hindi film number with a great conviction. Students and their teachers, side by side, on the same stage.

The statement was clear, unMute does not wait for an artist to be famous before giving them a proper stage. That is the whole point.

A Note from the Head of Talent Development

Four cities now. Each time we do this, someone in the room hears a student perform and looks genuinely surprised. That surprise is the whole point.
These are not kids who are almost ready. They are kids who showed up, put in the work, and earned the stage. Hyderabad was proof of that.
Hyderabad showed us, again, that the talent is everywhere. You just have to go find it. In July, we take unMute somewhere we have never been before. Watch this space.

About Artium unMute

unMute is Artium's live performance series. We hold one edition every two months, each in a different city. Performers are selected through an audition process, with priority given to students in the Artium Talent Development Programme.

Every set is professionally recorded and produced. Edited videos and reels release on Artium's YouTube and Instagram in the weeks after each edition.

The stage is earned. The camera is real. The audience is listening.
